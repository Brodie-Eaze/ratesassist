## Strategic positioning

Frame consistently across all touchpoints:

- We're **complementary**, not a replacement. RatesAssist sits *on* CiAnywhere, not next to it.
- We make **TechOne stickier** by adding AI productivity that customers will demand. Council CFOs increasingly ask their TechOne reps "where's your AI story?" — RatesAssist is that story.
- We're **AU-first, AU-hosted**, AU-compliant. Same posture as TechOne. No friction on data residency.
- We're **early enough to align**, but moving fast enough that TechOne's choice is to partner or to watch a partnership form somewhere else.

Avoid:
- Any framing that sounds competitive
- Implying TechOne's UX is poor (true, but not productive in this conversation)
- Over-promising customer counts
- Naming specific councils without their consent

---

## Fallback if TechOne slow-walks

The pilot proceeds via CSV export from the council's TechOne instance — cooperative, supported by mum's existing relationships. The recovery thesis can be validated and the first pilot signed without TechOne's API. Once revenue is flowing and customers are vocal, TechOne's posture typically shifts.

This isn't a threat to make in conversation. It's the contingency we run quietly while the partner conversation proceeds.
